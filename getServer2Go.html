Part One: Let the server connect to the players

Credit to EE & portforward.com

Explaination

This part makes a connection between one of your computer's 65536 ports (a road leading to YourComputerTown on Network Island) and one of the router's 65536 ports (a harbour on Network Island).

Step 1:

Go to your router and read the label. Write the circled password down:

Step 2:

Click here to access your router settings page. This will only work if you are connected to the router (you are).

Step 3:

Type in the username admin and the password you wrote down in Step 1. Press Login. Scribble out the password you wrote down in Step 1 (optional).

Step 4:

Press Windows Key + R to open Run, and paste in control ncpa.cpl and hit Enter. Right click on Ethernet, and click Properties. Don't change any check boxes, but select this:

then click Properties. If you see a dot in the circle beside "Obtain an ip address automatically", follow this guide (skipping steps 5 to 8), and copy down the ip address you assign. If not, copy the ip address in the IP address box. Close everything you opened in this step.

Step 5:

Go back to the Router Settings Page you opened in Step 2 and logged into in Step 3. Click the "Advanced" or "Advanced Settings" tab at the top.

Step 6:

Now click "NAT" in the menu on the left. Then click "Port Forwarding". This should bring up a new menu in the big space on the right.

Step 7:

In the first availible slot, fill in the following information:

  • LAN IP Address: Put in the final 2 digits of the IP address you wrote down in Step 4
  • Protocol Type: Select "TCP&UDP"
  • LAN Port / Private Ports: Enter 25565 in the box(es)
  • Public Port(s): Pick any number between 1024 and 65535 (NOT 25565 as a security precaution), and put it in the box(es). Also write it down.

Make sure you still have the IP address from Step 4 and the Public Port from Step 7!
